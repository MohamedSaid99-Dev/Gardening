Avocado

Avocado is a flowering tree likely originating from Mexico, generaly cultivated in tropical and mediteranean climates. Avocado is self-pollinating (breeding happens within the same plant). The fruit is also called avocado, it is incredibly nutritious with its heart-healthy fats and high amounts of potassium, vitamines (K, C, B6, B5, E), antioxidants and fibers.

How to Plant:

  • Once you've finished your avocado, wash and dry the pit
  • Fill a jar with water, almost to the brim.
  • Locate the broad end of the pit, it will point down to the water.
  • Stick three toothpicks around the pit. Place them so about one inch of the avocado pit is in the water.
  • Place the jar in a warm spot, out of direct sunlight.
  • Replenish water as needed
  • Your pit should start to sprout roots and a stem in two to six weeks
  • If you don't see any growth by eight weeks, start over with another pit
  • When the roots have grown thick and the stem has leaves, plant in soil in a pot about 20 cm in diameter, leaving half the seed still exposed above the soil
