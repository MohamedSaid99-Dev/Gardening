Dates

Date Palm is a flowering plant, cultivated for its edible sweet fruit, originating from the Middle East. It is widely cultivated across Northern Africa, the Middle East, the Horn of Africa and South Asia, and is naturalized in many tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. The date palm is dioecious, having separate male and female plants. They can be easily grown from seed, but only 50% of seedlings will be female and hence fruit bearing, and dates from seedling plants are often smaller and of poorer quality. Most commercial plantations thus use cuttings of heavily cropping cultivars.

How to Plant:

  • Start by finding a date you like.
  • Remove the seeds from the dates, and wash it.
  • Put the seeds in a cup of water.
  • Put the cup in a warm place away from sunlight, for 2 days.
  • Dampen a paper towel, Place the seeds on it and fold it over them.
  • Place them in a plastic bag and seal it completely.
  • Put the bag in a dark place, away from light.
  • Check the seeds every once in a while, so that they aren't dry.
  • if there's mold on a seed, remove the mold and try to lower humidity in the bag a bit.
  • After about 1-3 weeks roots should have appeared, now is a good time to plant them.
  • Fill a pot with soil and make a hole for the seed.
  • Place the seed in the hole carefully cover it with soil.
  • Keep watering the soil, keeping it humid and eventually your palm tree will grow.
